Dandelion Extraction Methods

Back to Dandelion Studies
Alcohol Tincture Hot Water Decoction Cold Infusion Dry Powder

1. Alcohol Tincture

Duration: 2–6 weeks Efficacy: High (broad-spectrum extract) Length: Shelf-stable 1–2 years

Best For: Extracting both water-soluble and alcohol-soluble compounds (bitter sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, phytosterols).

Steps:

  1. Harvest & Prepare:
    • Collect fresh dandelion roots (spring or late fall) or leaves/flowers (spring).
    • Rinse thoroughly, chop roots into small pieces (≈1 cm); roughly chop leaves/flowers.
    • Air-dry lightly (or use a dehydrator) until no surface moisture remains.
  2. Jar & Measure:
    • Fill a clean glass jar about halfway with chopped plant material.
    • Pour food-grade vodka (40–60 % ABV) until completely submerged, leaving ~1 cm headspace.
    • Seal tightly and tap to remove air bubbles.
  3. Infusion Period:
    • Store in a cool, dark place.
    • Shake gently once daily for 2–4 weeks (roots may require up to 6 weeks).
  4. Strain & Bottle:
    • Strain through fine mesh or cheesecloth into amber dropper bottles.
    • Squeeze residual liquid from herbs, discard spent material.
    • Label bottles with date and “Dandelion Tincture.”
  5. Dosage & Storage:
    • Typical dose: 20–40 drops (≈1 mL) 1–3× daily in water.
    • Store in a cool, dark place; keeps 1–2 years.

2. Hot Water Decoction

Duration: 20–30 minutes (roots); 10–15 minutes (leaves/flowers) Efficacy: Moderate (water-soluble compounds) Length: Fresh brew (use within 24 hours)

Best For: Extracting inulin, polysaccharides, some flavonoids. Good for a quick tonic or tea.

Steps:

  1. Harvest & Prepare:
    • Root: Clean and chop into small chunks (≈1 cm).
    • Leaves/Flowers: Roughly chop fresh or lightly dried.
  2. Simmer (Roots):
    • Combine 1 tbsp (≈5–7 g) dried root with 2 cups (500 mL) cold water.
    • Bring to a gentle boil, then simmer 20–30 minutes.
  3. Steep (Leaves/Flowers):
    • Place 1–2 tbsp (≈3–5 g) fresh or dried leaves/flowers in 1 cup (250 mL) boiling water.
    • Cover and steep 10–15 minutes.
  4. Strain & Drink:
    • Strain through fine mesh or coffee filter.
    • Drink 1 cup, 1–3× daily. Add lemon or honey to taste.
  5. Storage:
    • Refrigerate leftovers up to 24 hours; gently reheat before drinking.

3. Cold Infusion

Duration: 8–12 hours (refrigerator) Efficacy: Low-Moderate (delicate, water-soluble compounds) Length: 48 hours refrigerated

Best For: Preserving volatile, delicate compounds; mild bitterness; gentle tonic.

Steps:

  1. Prepare:
    • Chop fresh leaves or flowers; pack into a glass jar (halfway).
    • Cover completely with cold filtered water (leave ~1 cm headspace).
  2. Infuse:
    • Seal jar, place in refrigerator 8–12 hours (overnight).
    • Swirl gently once or twice during infusion.
  3. Strain & Use:
    • Strain and drink cold (½–1 cup at a time) or use in salad dressings.
  4. Storage:
    • Refrigerate; consume within 48 hours.

4. Dry Powder

Duration: Drying time 1–3 days Efficacy: High (full-spectrum, whole-plant) Length: 1–2 years (airtight storage)

Best For: Convenience, full spectrum of compounds; capsules, smoothies, tea stir-ins.

Steps:

  1. Harvest & Dry:
    • Harvest entire plants (roots, leaves, flowers). Rinse thoroughly.
    • Air-dry on a wire rack or use a dehydrator until brittle (1–3 days).
  2. Grind:
    • Use a clean coffee grinder or high-speed blender to pulverize to fine powder.
    • Sift through fine mesh to remove fibrous bits.
  3. Store & Use:
    • Store in airtight, light-proof container in a cool, dark pantry.
    • Typical dose: ½–1 tsp (≈1–2 g) daily, added to smoothies, capsules, or stirred into tea.

Quick Reference Metrics:

  • Alcohol Tincture: Duration 2–6 weeks | Efficacy High | Shelf-life 1–2 years
  • Hot Water Decoction: Duration 20–30 min (roots), 10–15 min (leaves) | Efficacy Moderate | Use within 24 hrs
  • Cold Infusion: Duration 8–12 hrs | Efficacy Low-Moderate | Use within 48 hrs
  • Dry Powder: Drying 1–3 days | Efficacy High | Shelf-life 1–2 years
