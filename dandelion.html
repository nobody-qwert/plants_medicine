Shittu et al. “Antioxidant and antimicrobial activities of Dandelion root extract (Taraxacum officinale) and its cytotoxic effect on MDA-MB-231 breast cancer cells” (2025) MDA-MB-231 human breast cancer cells (in vitro) Type: Aqueous Dandelion Root Extract (DRE)
Preparation: Fresh roots collected, shade-dried, powdered, macerated in distilled water, filtered, lyophilized into powder.
Concentrations Tested: 1.0, 2.5, 5.0, 10 mg/mL (reconstituted from lyophilized powder) 		24 h, 48 h, 72 h
  • IC₅₀ values (mg/mL):
    • 24 h: 4.472 mg/mL
    • 48 h: 4.167 mg/mL (↓ 6.8 % vs. 24 h)
    • 72 h: 3.450 mg/mL (↓ 17.2 % vs. 48 h)
  • Dose-dependent reduction in viability observed by trypan-blue exclusion; at 5 mg/mL, ~50 % cell death by 72 h.
  • Mild antioxidant activity by DPPH assay (IC₅₀ 2.860 ± 0.001 mg/mL).
 DRE exhibits a dose- and time-dependent cytotoxic effect on aggressive MDA-MB-231 cells, reducing viability by ~50 % at ≈4–5 mg/mL over 24–72 h. It also shows mild antioxidant activity but no antimicrobial effects against tested bacteria. These results support DRE’s potential as an anticancer agent targeting triple-negative breast cancer.
Ren et al. “Taraxasterol prompted the anti-tumor effect in mice burden hepatocellular carcinoma by regulating T lymphocytes” (Cell Death Discov., 2022)
  • HepG2 (human hepatoma; in vitro)
  • Huh7 (human hepatoma; in vitro)
  • H22 tumor-bearing mice (in vivo)
 Type: Purified Taraxasterol (TS) isolated from T. officinale roots
Preparation: Roots extracted (likely via ethanol/methanol), fractionated and purified by chromatography to isolate TS (purity ≥ 98 %).
Concentrations Tested:
• In vitro (µM): 5, 10, 15, 20, 25 µM
• In vivo (mg/kg, i.p.): 5 mg/kg and 7.5 mg/kg
  • In vitro: 24 – 120 h
  • In vivo: ~12 days (from tumor implantation to sacrifice)
  • In vitro (72 h; 15 µM):
    • HepG2 apoptosis rate: Control 6.48 % → TS 9.10 % (↑ 2.62 pp, p < 0.05)
    • Huh7 apoptosis rate: Control 14.17 % → TS 23.40 % (↑ 9.23 pp, p < 0.01)
    • Dose ≥ 20 µM: marked inhibition of proliferation from 48 h onward.
    • Colony formation reduced by ~50 % at 15 µM (p < 0.01).
  • In vivo (H22 mice):
    • TS 5 mg/kg: Tumor volume ↓ 32 % vs. control (p < 0.05)
    • TS 7.5 mg/kg: Tumor volume ↓ 52 % vs. control (p < 0.01)
    • Increased cleaved caspase-3 expression (↑ 2.1-fold, p < 0.01).
    • Ki-67 staining: Proliferation index ↓ 40 % (p < 0.01).
    • TS 7.5 mg/kg ↑ splenic CD4⁺ T cells by 31 % (p < 0.01); ↑ tumor-infiltrating CD4⁺/CD8⁺ T cells (p < 0.05).
TS significantly inhibits HCC proliferation, induces apoptosis (↑ caspase-3), causes G1 cell-cycle arrest, and reduces tumor growth by 32–52 % in H22-bearing mice. It also enhances anti-tumor immunity by increasing CD4⁺/CD8⁺ T-cell infiltration. These findings demonstrate TS’s robust antitumor efficacy against hepatocellular carcinoma.
Sigstedt et al. “Evaluation of aqueous extracts of Taraxacum officinale on growth and invasion of breast and prostate cancer cells” (International Journal of Oncology, 2008)
  • MCF-7/AZ (human breast cancer; in vitro)
  • LNCaP (human prostate cancer; in vitro)
 Type: Aqueous Dandelion Root Extract (DRE)
Preparation: Mature roots shade-dried, powdered, boiled in distilled water, filtered, used immediately (no further purification).
Concentration Tested: 50 µL/mL (~≈ 5 mg/mL of crude filtrate) 		48 h
  • Cell Viability: DRE (5 mg/mL) had no significant effect on proliferation of MCF-7/AZ or LNCaP cells at 48 h (p > 0.05).
  • Invasion Assay (Collagen I):
    • MCF-7/AZ invasion ↓ 38 % vs. control (p < 0.01).
    • LNCaP invasion unaffected by DRE (p > 0.05).
    Mechanism: DRE ↓ phospho-FAK by 45 % (p < 0.05) and ↓ MMP-9 activity by 52 % (p < 0.01) in MCF-7/AZ.
Aqueous root extract of T. officinale exhibited anti-invasive activity against breast cancer cells—blocking MCF-7/AZ invasion into collagen I—without affecting proliferation. This suggests DRE contains components that specifically inhibit metastatic potential via downregulation of FAK and MMP-9, indicating anti-invasive properties.
Clare et al. “The Diuretic Effect in Human Subjects of an Extract of Taraxacum officinale folium over a Single Day” (Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine, 2009) Healthy volunteers (n = 17; human, crossover design) Type: Fresh Leaf Hydroethanolic Extract (Folium)
Preparation: Fresh T. officinale leaves (1 g) extracted in 1 mL 50 % ethanol/50 % water (1 g:1 mL), shaken, filtered, standardized by TLC (chlorogenic acid marker).
Dose: 8 mL “Dandelion leaf extract” taken orally three times on Day 0 (total ~24 mL ≈ 24 g fresh leaf equivalent) 		Single day (measured in 5-h intervals)
  • Urination Frequency:
    • Baseline (Day –1, 8:00–13:00): 1.6 ± 0.3 episodes → After 1st dose (Day 0, same block): 2.3 ± 0.4 episodes (p < 0.05).
    • Day 0 (entire 24 h): 8.0 ± 0.8 → 9.0 ± 0.9 episodes (p < 0.05 vs. baseline).
  • Excretion Ratio (Urine Volume / Fluid Intake):
    • Baseline (13:00–18:00): 0.65 ± 0.07 → After 2nd dose: 0.92 ± 0.05 (p < 0.001).
    • 3rd dose produced no further change (p > 0.05).
A single day of hydroethanolic leaf extract (8 mL TID; ≈ 24 g fresh leaf) significantly increased urinary frequency (↑ 44 % in 5 h post-1st dose) and excretion ratio (↑ 42 % in 5 h post-2nd dose). The extract shows a mild but significant diuretic effect in healthy humans, warranting further investigation for clinical use.
